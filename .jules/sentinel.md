## 2024-04-06 - Prevent HTML and CRLF Injection in Email Templates
**Vulnerability:** User-provided inputs (`name`, `email`, `subject`, `message`) were inserted directly into HTML templates in `src/app/api/contact/route.ts` without sanitization. This allowed attackers to inject malicious HTML and XSS payloads into the contact form emails sent to the support team. Additionally, email headers (`subject`, `replyTo`) lacked sanitization, allowing for potential CRLF injection.
**Learning:** Even internal-facing emails (like contact form submissions) must sanitize all user inputs to prevent HTML/XSS injection on the admin/support side. The `sendEmail` utility handles structural parameters but does not automatically sanitize raw strings passed to the HTML body or headers.
**Prevention:** Always use `escapeHtml` (from `src/lib/html-sanitizer.ts`) when passing user inputs into HTML template bodies. Ensure email utility functions strip CRLF (`\r\n`) from header values (like `subject` or `replyTo`) using `stripNewlines` to prevent header injection.
