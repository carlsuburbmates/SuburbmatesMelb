## 2024-05-20 - Fix XSS Vulnerability in Contact Form Email Templating
**Vulnerability:** User inputs (name, email, subject, message) from the contact form were being interpolated directly into the HTML body of support emails without sanitization, leading to a potential HTML Injection/Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) vulnerability. Furthermore, the `subject` was being placed into the email header without sanitizing newlines, potentially causing email header injection.
**Learning:** Even internal-facing utilities like `sendEmail` which generate HTML must have their inputs explicitly sanitized before interpolation, as the base utility `sendEmail` does not auto-sanitize inputs.
**Prevention:** Created a standard sanitization utility (`src/lib/html-sanitizer.ts`) containing `escapeHtml` and `stripNewlines` and required its use for any variable interpolation inside an HTML email string template. Email subjects (in the email header) must be sanitized with `stripNewlines` to avoid newlines but avoid double encoding with `escapeHtml`. However, if the subject is also interpolated into the HTML email body, it must be wrapped in `escapeHtml` at the interpolation site.
